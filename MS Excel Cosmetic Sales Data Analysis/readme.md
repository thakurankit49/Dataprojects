A dynamic, interactive Excel dashboard designed to track, analyze, and visualize cosmetics sales performance across different timeframes, geographic regions, products, and sales personnel. This tool transforms raw transactional data into actionable insights for business stakeholders.

 Dashboard Overview:
 
The dashboard is structured into two main components: an interactive Control Panel (Slicers) on the left and four key Performance Visualizations on the right.

Interactive Slicers:

1. Users can filter the entire dashboard globally with a single click using the following parameters:

2. Sales Person: Filter performance by individual team members (e.g., Ananya Gupta, Ava Sharma, Ethan Reddy, etc.).

3. Product: Drill down into specific product performance (e.g., Aloe Vera Gel, Anti-Aging Serum, Charcoal Face Wash, etc.).

4. Month: Narrow the analysis down to specific months of the year (January through August).

Visualizations & Key Metrics:

1. Total Sales By Months (Top Left)
Type: Line Chart with Data Labels.

Purpose: Tracks revenue trends over time to identify seasonal peaks and valleys.

Insight: Highlights a significant sales surge in March ($484,102) followed by a gradual tapering off through August ($275,299).

2. Total Sales by Countries (Top Right)
Type: Clustered Column Chart.

Purpose: Compares market performance across international regions.

Insight: Identifies geographic strongholds, showing the USA ($628,488) and New Zealand ($557,060) as the top-performing markets.

3. Total Sales by Sales Person (Bottom Left)
Type: Horizontal Bar Chart (Sorted).

Purpose: Monitors individual sales team performance and identifies top contributors.

Insight: Benchmarks team members, highlighting Olivia D'Souza ($387,406) and Sophia Nair ($319,888) as the leading sales representatives.

4. Boxes Shipped by Month (Bottom Right)
Type: Trend Line Chart.

Purpose: Monitors logistics and supply chain volume, mapping physical distribution against sales revenue.

Insight: Corresponds closely with the revenue chart, peaking in March with 15,167 boxes shipped.
