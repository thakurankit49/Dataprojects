The goal of this project is to analyze corporate workforce data to identify the key drivers of employee attrition. By utilizing complex SQL queries, I investigated correlations between turnover and factors such as income brackets, age groups, overtime, and work-life balance to provide actionable insights for HR retention strategies.

Technical Skills Demonstrated:

1. Aggregate Functions: Extensive use of SUM, COUNT, AVG, and MIN/MAX for high-level reporting.

2. Conditional Logic: Implementing CASE WHEN statements to calculate custom attrition rates and percentages.

3. Data Binning: Grouping continuous variables (Age, Monthly Income) into categorical brackets to identify high-risk segments.

4. Subqueries: Using nested queries to compare individual employee data against company-wide averages.

5. Filtering & Sorting: Leveraging HAVING, TOP N, and ORDER BY to isolate critical organizational pain points.

Key Business Questions Explored:

1. Workforce Demographics - gender distribution and average age across the organization along with departments and job roles having the highest average salaries

2. Attrition Drivers (The "Why") - Comparing the attrition rate of employees who work overtime vs. those who do not.
and analyzing attrition rates across different income brackets. Determining the average total working years for employees who leave versus those who stay.

3. Organizational Risk Factors Job Roles: Identifying the Top 3 job roles with the highest turnover percentages. Work-Life Balance: Correlating employee retention with reported work-life balance scores. Loyalty: Tracking employees with 5+ years in their current role who remain with the company.
